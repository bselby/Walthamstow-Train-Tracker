# Walthamstow Train Tracker — Design Context

## Users

One primary user: Ben, walking over East Avenue bridge in Walthamstow with his toddler. **Primary usage is daytime, outdoors, on a phone, one-handed while pushing a buggy.** The toddler actively looks at the screen — so the interface has two audiences with different needs (parent needs fast data, toddler needs something visually engaging).

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** *bright · cheerful · railway-truthful*

- **Bright:** optimised for daylight, confident colour, no gloom.
- **Cheerful:** the app is about a small daily joy (watching trains with a toddler). It should feel like that, not like a utility.
- **Railway-truthful:** data-first. The countdown and the train position are the heroes. Decoration serves them, not the other way around. No fluff, no irony, no "AI aesthetic."

## Aesthetic Direction

Think: a children's book about London trains, printed on warm off-white paper, with confident type and a handful of saturated colours. Not: a transit dashboard. Not: gradient SaaS. Not: dark mode + cyan accents (the current state — explicitly being rejected).

- **Light theme, daylight-optimised** — must survive on a phone in bright sun.
- **Data is the hero** — countdown numbers are the biggest, boldest thing on screen. Everything else supports them.
- **Playful without being cutesy** — round-edged, warm, but not infantile. A toddler should want to look at it; an adult shouldn't feel embarrassed holding it up.
- **Real-world palette** — London's actual rail branding (Overground/Weaver orange) is on the table as a starting reference; so are the primary colours of children's picture books.

## Anti-references

- The current dark-mode + cyan implementation (we built it, now we're redoing it).
- TfL's official app (corporate, crowded, adult-transit).
- Grey-on-grey SaaS dashboards.
- Web3 / gradient text / neon on black.
- Twee, baby-food-styled design — the app should have quality and restraint, just in a cheerful key.

## Accessibility

- Must be readable in direct sunlight (high contrast even on light bg).
- Tabular-nums on countdowns so digits don't shift while ticking.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` already supported — keep it.
- Zoomable to 200%, tap targets where relevant sized for buggy-pushing one-handed use.

## Design Principles

1. **The countdown is sacred.** It's the largest, highest-contrast, clearest element on every screen. Everything else serves it.
2. **Delight earns its keep.** Animations, illustrations, colour — they must support readability and emotional warmth, never compete with the data.
3. **One memorable thing.** Not a thousand little details — one bold choice (typeface, colour, illustration, motion) that you remember after closing the app.
4. **Daylight first.** Designed, tested, and tuned for outdoor mobile use in British daylight.
